Zendesk, HubSpot, & Salesforce: The Modern CS Integration Guide

Your CS platform is only as good as the data it can see. Here is how to build a unified view of your customers without the "integration tax."

In modern SaaS, customer success is a team sport. Your support team uses Zendesk, your sales team uses Salesforce or HubSpot, and your CS team is often left trying to bridge the gap.

Choosing a CS platform shouldn't mean adding another data silo. It should mean unifying your existing ones. Here is how Renewal360 integrates with your core stack to create a "Customer 360" view.

The CRM Connection: Salesforce & HubSpot

A CS platform needs to know who the customer is, what they pay, and when they renew. Renewal360 uses OAuth-based integrations with Salesforce and HubSpot to pull this data automatically.

  • Salesforce: Map any custom object or field to drive health scores.
  • HubSpot: Sync contacts and deal stages in real time.

The Support Signal: Zendesk

Support tickets are often the earliest indicator of churn. If a customer has three open "High Priority" tickets, they shouldn't be receiving a "Happy Renewal" email.

Renewal360's **Zendesk integration** does more than just show tickets. It analyzes ticket sentiment and volume to automatically adjust the account's health score. If support load is high, the system flags the CSM and can even pause automated outreach until the issues are resolved.

Most integrations fail because they are "one-way syncs" that create stale data. Renewal360 uses **bi-directional intelligence**. We don't just read your CRM; we use those signals to drive actions in the real world—sending emails, booking meetings on Google/Microsoft calendars, and alerting your team in Slack.
