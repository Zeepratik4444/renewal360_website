What do the first 7 days in Renewal360 actually look like?

The 7-day setup timeline is not marketing shorthand - it reflects how the integrations are actually structured. Here is what happens:

Days 1–2

CRM and support desk connected via OAuth

Salesforce or HubSpot connects in minutes. Account data, ARR values, renewal dates, and contact records sync automatically. Zendesk connects on day 2 - ticket history and sentiment analysis activates immediately across your account base.

Day 3

First renewal Journey configured in the Visual Builder

Use the pre-built T-90/60/30 template or build your own branching logic. Define what triggers the high-risk escalation branch versus the standard renewal track. This takes 1–2 hours, not weeks - no code, no RevOps admin required.

Days 4–5

AI begins drafting outreach for accounts in the renewal window

Every account inside your configured renewal window has a personalised draft email generated using real account context - CRM data, Zendesk sentiment, usage signals, and prior email threads. Drafts appear in the Approval Queue for CSM review.

Days 6–7

First emails approved and sent, reply monitoring active

CSMs review the Approval Queue - typically 10 minutes per day. Approved emails send with the CSM's signature. IMAP monitoring is live from the moment the first email goes out - any customer reply pauses the sequence immediately and sends a Slack alert.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Renewal360 scale to handle a growing CS team as we hire?

Yes. The platform supports multiple CSMs, manager-level visibility across all accounts, role-based access controls, and account reassignment workflows. As your team grows from 1 CSM to 10, the automation layer scales with you - each new CSM gets their own approval queue and account assignments without additional configuration.

We already use HubSpot Sequences - why do we need Renewal360?

HubSpot Sequences are linear drip tools - they send on a schedule without branching logic, health score integration, reply detection, or AI drafting. Renewal360 adds branching sequences that adapt based on account health changes, pause automatically when a customer replies, and draft each email with real account context rather than generic merge fields. The two tools serve different purposes.

Does Renewal360 write back to our CRM, or is it read-only?

Bi-directional. Renewal360 reads your CRM data continuously and writes outreach activity - emails sent, sequence status, health score changes - back to Salesforce or HubSpot contact records automatically. Your sales team's CRM view stays accurate without requiring them to use Renewal360 directly.

What happens if a CSM leaves and their accounts need to be reassigned?

Account reassignment in Renewal360 is a one-click operation from the admin dashboard. The new CSM inherits the account's active sequences, health score history, and approval queue. Sequences that were mid-flight can be paused, continued, or restarted depending on where the account is in its renewal lifecycle.
