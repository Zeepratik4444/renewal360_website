Rethinking
pricing around
upgrade pull

Each plan tier should feel like an obvious next step — not a forced choice. This refactor maps features to the actual product, creates clear value jumps, and removes noise that doesn't convert.

01 No seat limits, no API access, no Enterprise tier. Unlimited seats at every tier. Clean 4-plan structure: Free → SMB → Growth → Scale.
02 Active Accounts is the core usage lever. It's the most intuitive metric — users understand it immediately and it scales with business size.
03 Each jump must feel like a capability unlock, not just "more". The Growth tier is the star — it's where the product becomes an intelligence platform.
★ Every new signup gets 7 days of Scale — then drops to Free. Users experience the full product before hitting any limits. The trial ends with a clear downgrade moment designed to convert.
The Upgrade Story
Free → SMB
The Operational Jump
CSMs move from manually managing 25 accounts to automating 200 with smarter AI and CRM sync. The day-to-day gets dramatically faster.
Trigger moment "We've outgrown 25 accounts and need HubSpot connected + Smart AI handling objections."

Trial Strategy

7 days of Scale,
then Free.

Every new signup experiences the full product — unlimited accounts, NPS surveys, Revenue at Risk, weighted health scoring, Salesforce, dedicated features. Then on Day 8, they drop to Free. The downgrade moment is the conversion trigger.

Day
1
Signup → Instant Scale Access
User onboards with unlimited accounts, full CRM integrations, and all AI intelligence features active. No credit card required.
Day
5
In-app Warning Banner
"Your Scale trial ends in 2 days. Upgrade to keep Revenue at Risk, NPS Surveys, and Weighted Health Scoring." — with a direct upgrade CTA.
Day
7
Trial Expiry Email
"Your Scale trial ends tonight." — summary of what they've set up, what they'll lose, and the one-click upgrade link. Sent at 9am user time.
Day
8
Downgrade to Free — The Conversion Moment
User is now on Free (25 accounts, basic features). The delta between what they had and what they have now is the sales pitch. Upgrade prompt shown on every locked feature.
What a user loses on Day 8 (Free vs Scale)
📊
Revenue at Risk Dashboard
The ARR visibility they saw during trial disappears. Executives who were shown this will feel the loss immediately.
🧮
Weighted Health Scoring
Reverts to basic risk flags only. Users who trusted the 5-factor score for 7 days won't trust basic back.
⭐
NPS Surveys + CRM Sync
NPS locked, HubSpot/Salesforce disconnected, account ceiling drops to 25. Very hard to ignore.
Conversion Nudge — Recommended Plan to Upsell to
On Day 8, don't present all 4 plans equally. Default the upgrade modal to highlight Growth — it's ₹49K vs ₹99K, and it includes all the intelligence features they used during the trial (NPS, Revenue at Risk, Weighted Scoring). Only users with Salesforce needs will self-select Scale. This maximises conversion volume while Scale self-selects naturally.

What We're Removing & Why
✕
Enterprise Tier — 4 tiers with Scale already covering large teams
Adds sales complexity without clear differentiation from Scale. Removes it entirely.
✕
API Access — internal architecture, not a customer-facing feature
Kept internal. Not exposed as a plan benefit — avoids misaligned expectations.
✕
Seat / User Limits — friction with no real revenue upside at this stage
Unlimited seats at all tiers. CS tools need the whole team in — limiting seats blocks adoption.
✕
Account Add-ons (₹35K/500 accounts) — complicates the price conversation
Clean account tiers eliminate the "math before buying" problem. Scale to next tier instead.
Strategic Rationale
Growth is the star tier
NPS Surveys, Revenue at Risk Dashboard, and 5-Factor Weighted Health Scoring are all in the Growth plan. These are the three features from the feature roadmap that transform the product from a "renewal tracker" to a "churn prediction platform." Executives want these — and they're the ones who approve the budget jump from SMB.
SMB closes the adoption gap
Smart AI objection handling unlocks at SMB — the product already has this. Keeping "Basic" handling on Free creates a real, tangible quality difference without gate-keeping core function. HubSpot + Slack on SMB gives the CSM team a genuinely faster workflow, not just more accounts.
Scale reserves the relationship
Salesforce and Pipedrive integrations, a Dedicated Success Manager, and Priority Slack support sit at Scale. This creates a clear value add beyond just "unlimited accounts" — it's the tier for companies that want a partner, not just software.
Free is a real pilot, not a preview
25 accounts with AI Renewal Sequences, Email Approval Workflow, and basic Health Scoring is enough to run a genuine cohort and see real results. Most trial users will hit the 25-account ceiling and upgrade naturally — no artificial restrictions needed to force the hand.
